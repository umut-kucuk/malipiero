Gian Francesco Malipiero
Venezia, 18 March 1882 – 1 August 1973

Project Overview

Gian Francesco Malipiero was a highly influential Italian composer who lived from 1882 to 1973. He was born in Venice, Italy, and spent much of his life exploring and experimenting with various musical styles and techniques. Malipiero's works often incorporate elements of folk music and are characterized by their intricate harmonies and unconventional forms. He was also known for his extensive research into the music of the Italian Renaissance and Baroque periods, which heavily influenced his own compositions. Malipiero's contributions to the world of music were significant, both as a composer and a musicologist. This digital archive aims to shed light on Malipiero's life and works, providing an in-depth look at his compositions, his musical philosophy, and his lasting legacy in the world of classical music. This study represents a preliminary stage of a larger project that may be developed in the future. As a proof-of-concept, it serves as a demonstration of the project's feasibility and should not be regarded as a completed project.

The archive contains three main types of content: textual documents, embedded videos, and network data visualization. The textual documents include a collection of primary and secondary sources related to Malipiero, such as reviews, and music scores. The embedded videos feature performances, interviews, and documentaries related to Malipiero's music and life. Finally, the network data visualization is an interactive visualization of the relationships and connections between different elements related to Malipiero, such as his co-author relationships, works, and education.

Data Sources

Musicbrainz

The API discussed here is an interface to the MusicBrainz Database. It is aimed at developers of media players, CD rippers, taggers, and other applications requiring music metadata. The API's architecture follows the REST design principles. Interaction with the API is done using HTTP and all content is served in a simple but flexible format, in either XML or JSON. XML is the default format; to get a JSON response, you can either set the Accept header to "application/json" or add fmt=json to the query string

Data extraction was performed with the help of Musicbrainz API and Python, and the process was detailed in the Jupyternotebook. This jupyternotebook is available on github.

The obtained data was visualized using the d3.js library and a network type data visualization was created. The obtained data was visualized using the d3.js library and a network type data visualization was created. It is possible to access the visualization and its codes through Observable.
Wikidata

Wikidata is a free, collaborative, multilingual, secondary database, collecting structured data to provide support for Wikipedia, Wikimedia Commons, the other wikis of the Wikimedia movement, and to anyone in the world. The Wikidata repository consists mainly of items, each one having a label, a description and any number of aliases. Items are uniquely identified by a Q followed by a number, such as Douglas Adams (Q42).

Data about Malipiero on Wikidata were queried with SPARQL and the results were obtained in JSON format. The data was then filtered just like the data from Musicbrainz.

The data obtained from the Wikidata Sparql endpoint is then mashed with the previously obtained data and the resulting result is visualized.
Europeana

Europeana is a web portal created by the European Union containing digitised cultural heritage collections of more than 3,000 institutions across Europe.

Europeana had its beginnings after a letter was jointly sent in April 2005 by Jacques Chirac, President of France, and the premiers of Germany, Spain, Italy, Poland and Hungary to the President of the European Commission, José Manuel Durão Barroso. It urged the creation of a virtual European library in order to make Europe's cultural heritage more accessible to everyone.

Non-copyrighted videos about Malipiero from Europana, which acts as the aggregator, are included in the archive.

Maestro Roberto Zarpellon

Roberto Zarpellon started attending the Academy of Music and Fine Arts in Vienna in 1983, and he graduated in Organ and Organ Composition at the Conservatory "S. Cecilia" in Rome in 1985. He later received a degree from the Academy of Vienna in 1988, where he studied with Professor Alfred Mitterhofer. Zarpellon has conducted various orchestras and performances across Europe, the United States, Brazil, and Japan. He has also recorded with renowned record labels such as DG, FraBernardo, and Nuova Era. Zarpellon is the founder of the "Lorenzo Da Ponte" Chamber Orchestra, and he is a professor of Orchestra at the Conservatory of Castelfranco Veneto. He has written essays and publications, including "La musica degli Affetti," and has worked as a consultant for the Italian Ministry of Cultural Heritage.
